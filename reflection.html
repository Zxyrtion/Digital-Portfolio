Chapter 04

Personal Reflection

Critical insights on digital rights, the evolving landscape of online expression, and personal perspectives on the future of freedom in the digital age.

What are the limits of freedom of expression online?

Freedom of expression online is broad, but it has limits. It stops when a person’s words harm others—such as through defamation, harassment, encouraging violence, or spreading false and harmful information. In the Philippines, some of these limits are included in Republic Act No. 10175, but the law has been criticized for being too broad, especially in cases like cyber libel. In simple terms, freedom of expression should only be limited when it harms others. If what you say causes damage and is not based on facts, then it goes beyond the boundary of free expression.

Should there be stricter cybercrime laws?

The answer is not simple. In serious cases like child exploitation, large-scale fraud, and government cyberattacks, stronger laws may be needed because current punishments may not be enough. However, when it comes to speech and expression, stricter laws should be handled very carefully. Experience shows that unclear or overly broad cybercrime laws can be misused to silence people who speak out or tell the truth. Instead of just making laws stricter, the priority should be to make them clearer and fairer.

How did this project change your perspective about using social media?

This project changed how I see myself online. Before, I thought social media was just a place to share my opinions freely without serious consequences. Now, I understand that everything I post or share has legal and ethical importance. Because of this, I have become more careful about what I post, more aware of people who are bullied online, and more mindful of the thin line between freedom of expression and abuse.

Should freedom of expression online be limited?

Yes—but it must be done carefully, openly, and with proper oversight. Unlimited freedom of expression online can sometimes lead to real harm, such as harassment, fake information, radicalization, and abuse of vulnerable groups. Because of this, some regulation may be necessary and justified. However, rules about online expression should not give too much power to governments or companies. These limits must be clearly written in law, fair to the situation, reviewed by courts, and should never be used to silence political opinions.
