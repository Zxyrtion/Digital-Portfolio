Chapter 01

Conceptual Foundations

Understanding the legal and philosophical bedrock of freedom of expression in the Philippines and the cybercrime laws that define its boundaries.

1987 Philippine Constitution

Article III, Section 4 guarantees that "No law shall be passed abridging the freedom of speech, of expression, or of the press, or the right of the people peaceably to assemble and petition the government for redress of grievances."

This provision is the cornerstone of democratic discourse in the Philippines, protecting citizens' right to voice opinions, criticize the government, and participate in public debate — including online platforms.

  • Protects political speech
  • Covers press and media freedom
  • Includes peaceful assembly
  • Applies to digital expression

RA 10175 — Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012

Signed into law on September 12, 2012, RA 10175 addresses offenses committed through information and communications technology. It criminalizes acts such as hacking, identity theft, cybersex, and notably — cyber libel.

Section 4(c)(4) penalizes online libel with imprisonment of prision correccional in its maximum period to prision mayor in its minimum period — a penalty one degree higher than ordinary libel.

  • Covers cyber libel & online defamation
  • Addresses hacking & unauthorized access
  • Penalizes cybersex & child pornography
  • Includes identity theft provisions
Key Terms

Legal Glossary

Essential terms you need to understand when navigating digital expression and cybercrime law.

Libel

A written or published false statement that damages a person's reputation. Under the Revised Penal Code, it requires a defamatory imputation, malice, publication, and an identifiable victim.

Cyber Libel

Libel committed through a computer system or other similar means. Carries a heavier penalty than traditional libel and has a longer prescriptive period of 15 years.

Privacy

The right to be free from unwarranted intrusion. Online, this includes protection from unauthorized data collection, surveillance, and exposure of personal information.

Online Harassment

Repeated, unwanted contact or behavior online intended to intimidate, threaten, or distress a person. May constitute cyberbullying or unjust vexation under Philippine law.

Hate Speech

Speech that attacks individuals or groups based on attributes such as race, religion, ethnicity, or gender. While not explicitly defined in RA 10175, it may fall under other penal provisions.

Data Privacy

Governed by RA 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012), this protects personal information collected, stored, and processed by organizations and individuals online.
